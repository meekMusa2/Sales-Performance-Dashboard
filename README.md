# 📊 Sales Performance Dashboard

**Power BI | Microsoft Excel | DAX | Data Visualisation**

> A 3-page interactive sales analytics dashboard built using the Superstore dataset

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyses retail sales data from a US-based superstore covering the period **2014 – 2017**. The goal was to build an interactive Power BI dashboard that helps business stakeholders understand revenue trends, product performance, and regional/customer distribution at a glance.

---

## 🎯 Business Questions Answered

- What is the overall revenue, profit, and profit margin?
- Which months and quarters drive the most sales?
- Which product categories and sub-categories perform best?
- Which states and regions generate the most revenue?
- Who are the top 10 customers by sales?
- Where are discounts hurting profitability?

---

## 📐 Dashboard Pages

### Page 1 — Overview
High-level KPIs and trend analysis
- Total Revenue, Total Profit, Profit Margin %, Total Orders, Average Order Value
- Monthly Revenue Trend (Line Chart)
- Sales by Category (Bar Chart)
- Sales by Region (Donut Chart)
- Sales by Segment (Donut Chart)

### Page 2 — Product Analysis
Deep dive into product and category performance
- Top 10 Products by Sales (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- Sales by Sub-Category (Bar Chart)
- Profit vs Discount Analysis (Scatter Plot)

### Page 3 — Regional & Customer Analysis
Geographic and customer breakdown
- Sales by State (Map Visual)
- Top 10 States by Sales (Bar Chart)
- Top 10 Customers by Sales (Bar Chart)
- Revenue by Segment (Treemap)

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- **Technology** is the highest revenue category, followed closely by Furniture
- **California, New York, and Texas** are the top 3 states by revenue
- **Consumer segment** accounts for the largest share of revenue (approx. 51%)
- **High discounts (40%+) consistently result in negative profit** — a key business risk
- **Canon imageCLASS 2200** is the single highest revenue-generating product at $62K
- Revenue shows a clear **upward trend** from 2014 to 2017 with seasonal peaks in Q4
